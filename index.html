Projects


Chemical Contaimination Heatmap of Michigan

Link Link

As both team member and later, project leader for this project, Emily and her team found open source data from the EPA and used Python libraries to clean the data and create the heatmap. From there, HTML, CSS, and JSON were utilized to create an interactive heatmap and website that displays multiple contaiminant and the zipcodes affected. The final product serves as a tool for Michiganders to understand pollution levels in their hometowns and increases awareness on the chemical contaiminants that are unfortunately becoming harder and hard to avoid, without actions from environmental activists and policy makers.

See the full writeup on Michigan EcoData's website!


Potential Urban Farm Mapping of Detroit

Link Link

Many areas of Detroit, due to socioeconomic, demographic and/or other reasons, are at risk of being in a food desert. Many activists already have started and maintained urban farms all around Detroit. Using ArcGIS, HTML, and open source Geographical Information Systems (GIS) data from the city of Detroit, Emily and Madeline Richards' team created multiple GIS maps with demographic, vacant land, soil, neighborhood revitalization, and existing urban farm data to pinpoint the best locations for new urban farms in Detroit and other Michigan cities.

Eigenvalues and Population Dynamics

Link

Emily, along with her peers, were able to model long-term steady state population dynamics by finding eigenvalues and eigenvectors of transition and Leslie matrices. By using emmigration and immigration data between Beligium and the Netherlands in 2017, they were able to determine the final long-term populations of the two countries, as well as the final population of blue monkeys in Kenya with the birth and death rate data over a 29 year period.

Learning to Code with Scratch: STEM Society Lesson

With STEM Society at the University of Michigan, Emily and her team developed and taught a beginner's coding lesson with Scratch, a high-level block-based programming language targeted for young adults and children. The capstone event, STEM Saturday, where the lesson was taught, aims to get under-represented groups in STEM fields excited about specialized and interactive topics with current STEM students at the University of Michigan.
